* **Plaintext:** This is your original message - the text or data that you want to keep secret.  It's readable by anyone who sees it. (e.g., "Meet me at the park.")

**3. The Process – How Encryption Actually Works**

Encryption uses mathematical algorithms (complex sets of rules) and keys to transform plaintext into ciphertext. Here's a simplified breakdown:

* **Algorithm:** This is the *method* used for encryption. There are many different algorithms, like AES (Advanced Encryption Standard), RSA, and others. Each algorithm has its own way of scrambling data.
* **Key:** This is a secret piece of information – usually a number – that’s used by the algorithm to perform the encryption.  The key is absolutely crucial! * **Encryption Process:** The algorithm takes the plaintext and the key as input. It then performs a series of calculations based on those inputs, transforming the plaintext into ciphertext.



**4. Decryption – Getting it Back!**

Decryption is essentially the reverse process.  It uses *another* key (usually the same one used for encryption) and the *same algorithm* to convert the ciphertext back into readable plaintext. 


**5. Types of Encryption**

There are two main types:

* **Symmetric-Key Encryption:** This is the most common type. The same key is used for both encryption and decryption.  It's fast and efficient, but you need a secure way to share the key with everyone who needs access. (Examples: AES)
* **Asymmetric-Key Encryption (Public Key Cryptography):** Uses separate keys – a public key (which anyone can have) and a private key (which only you should know).
