# Constraint Optimization Report

**Problem:** A bakery produces artisan bread and croissants. Each loaf of bread requires 3 hours of labor and 2 kg of flour, earning $12 profit. Each batch of croissants requires 1 hour of labor and 3 kg of flour, earning $8 profit. The bakery has 12 hours of labor and 15 kg of flour available each day. Both products must have non-negative quantities. Maximize daily profit.

**Solver status:** `Optimal`
**Optimal objective value:** 60

**Decision variables:**
  - bread = 3
  - croissants = 3

## Interpretation

**Optimal Decision:** Produce 3 loaves of bread and 3 batches of croissants daily.

**Optimal Profit:** $60 per day ($36 from bread, $24 from croissants).

**Binding Constraints:** Both constraints are fully used — the plan consumes all 12 hours of labor (3×3 + 3×1) and all 15 kg of flour (3×2 + 3×3). There is zero slack in either resource.

**Recommendation:** Since both labor and flour are completely exhausted at the optimum, acquiring even a small amount of additional labor or flour would directly increase profit — prioritize whichever resource is cheaper to expand.

## Solver Code (LLM-generated, PuLP)

```python
prob = pulp.LpProblem("bakery_profit", pulp.LpMaximize)

bread = pulp.LpVariable("bread", lowBound=0)
croissants = pulp.LpVariable("croissants", lowBound=0)

prob += 12 * bread + 8 * croissants

prob += 3 * bread + croissants <= 12
prob += 2 * bread + 3 * croissants <= 15

prob.solve(pulp.PULP_CBC_CMD(msg=0))

_result = {
    "status": pulp.LpStatus[prob.status],
    "objective": pulp.value(prob.objective),
    "variables": {v.name: pulp.value(v) for v in prob.variables()}
}
```
