# Constraint Optimization Report

**Problem:** A bakery produces artisan bread and croissants. Each loaf of bread requires 3 hours of labor and 2 kg of flour, earning $12 profit. Each batch of croissants requires 1 hour of labor and 3 kg of flour, earning $8 profit. The bakery has 12 hours of labor and 15 kg of flour available each day. Both products must have non-negative quantities. Maximize daily profit.

**Solver status:** `Optimal`
**Optimal objective value:** 60

**Decision variables:**
  - Bread = 3
  - Croissants = 3

## Interpretation

To maximize daily profit, the bakery should produce **3 loaves of bread** and **3 batches of croissants**. This combination yields a maximum total profit of **$60.00**.

Crucially, both available resources are fully utilized: all 12 hours of labor and all 15 kg of flour will be used. Because both the labor and flour constraints are binding, the bakery is operating at full capacity. To increase profit further, management must acquire more time (labor) or more raw materials (flour).

## Solver Code (LLM-generated, PuLP)

```python
prob = pulp.LpProblem("Bakery Profit Maximization", pulp.LpMaximize)

# Define decision variables (Bread and Croissants quantities)
bread = pulp.LpVariable("Bread", lowBound=0)
croissants = pulp.LpVariable("Croissants", lowBound=0)

# Set the objective function: Maximize Profit (12*B + 8*C)
prob += 12 * bread + 8 * croissants, "Total Profit"

# Add constraints
# Labor constraint: 3*B + 1*C <= 12
prob += 3 * bread + 1 * croissants <= 12, "Labor Constraint"

# Flour constraint: 2*B + 3*C <= 15
prob += 2 * bread + 3 * croissants <= 15, "Flour Constraint"

# Solve the problem
prob.solve(pulp.PULP_CBC_CMD(msg=0))

# Store results exactly as specified
_result = {
    "status": pulp.LpStatus[prob.status],
    "objective": pulp.value(prob.objective),
    "variables": {v.name: pulp.value(v) for v in prob.variables()}
}
```
