Neural networks are a fundamental concept in machine learning and artificial
  intelligence. In simple terms, they are computational models inspired by
  the human brain's structure and function.

Imagine a network of interconnected nodes or "neurons" that process and
  transmit information. Each node receives one or more inputs, performs a
  computation on those inputs, and then sends the output to other nodes.
  This process allows the network to learn and represent complex patterns in
  data.

There are two main types of neural networks: feedforward and recurrent.

* Feedforward networks are simple and straightforward, where each node
  processes its inputs directly without any feedback loops.
* Recurrent networks are more complex, as they allow the nodes to receive
  feedback from previous outputs. This enables them to learn sequences and
  temporal relationships in data.

Neural networks can be trained using various algorithms, such as
  backpropagation or gradient descent. The training process involves
  adjusting the weights and biases of each node to minimize the error
  between predicted and actual output values.
