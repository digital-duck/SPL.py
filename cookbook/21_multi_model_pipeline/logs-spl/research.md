**1. The Science – What is Climate Change?**

* **Greenhouse Effect:** The Earth's atmosphere naturally traps some heat from the sun, keeping it warm enough to support life. This is known as the greenhouse effect. Certain gases in the atmosphere (carbon dioxide, methane, nitrous oxide) are key to this process.
* **Human Influence:** Since the Industrial Revolution (around 1750), human activities – primarily burning fossil fuels (coal, oil, and natural gas) for energy – have dramatically increased the concentration of these greenhouse gases in the atmosphere. This is intensifying the greenhouse effect.
* **Observed Changes:** The planet is warming, and this warming is unequivocally linked to human activity. 


**2. Key Statistics & Data (as of late 2023)**

* **Global Temperature Rise:**  The Earth’s average surface temperature has risen by approximately 1.1°C (2°F) since the pre-industrial era (1850-1900). The vast majority of this warming occurred in recent decades.
* **Rate of Warming:** The last decade (2014-2023) was the warmest on record, with each year exceeding its predecessor.  The rate of warming is accelerating.
* **CO2 Levels:** Atmospheric CO2 concentrations are at their highest levels in at least 800,000 years - roughly 419 parts per million (ppm) as of November 2023. This is a significant increase from pre-industrial levels of around 280 ppm.
* **Sea Level Rise:** Global mean sea level has risen by about 21-27 centimeters (8-10 inches) since 1880, primarily due to thermal expansion of water as it warms and the melting of glaciers and ice sheets.  The rate of rise is accelerating.
* **Arctic Sea Ice Decline:** Arctic sea ice extent has declined by approximately 40% since 1979. Summer sea ice minimums have fallen dramatically.


**3. Observed Impacts – What’s Happening Now?**

* **Extreme Weather Events:** Increased frequency and/or intensity of:
    * **Heatwaves:**  Globally, there has been a significant increase in the frequency and duration of heatwaves.
    * **Heavy Precipitation & Flooding:** Warmer air holds more moisture, leading to heavier rainfall events and increased flood risk. 
    * **Droughts:** Altered precipitation patterns are contributing to prolonged droughts in many regions.
    * **Wildfires:** Higher temperatures and drier conditions are fueling larger and more intense wildfires globally (particularly in the Western US, Australia, and Mediterranean).
    * **Hurricanes/Tropical Cyclones:** While there's debate about overall frequency changes, climate change is intensifying hurricanes by increasing ocean temperatures and contributing to heavier rainfall.
* **Melting Ice & Glaciers:**  Massive ice loss from Greenland and Antarctic ice sheets, as well as glaciers worldwide, contributes to sea level rise.
* **Ocean Acidification:** The absorption of excess CO2 by the oceans is causing them to become more acidic, threatening marine ecosystems (coral reefs, shellfish).
* **Shifting Ecosystems:** Species are shifting their ranges in response to changing temperatures and precipitation patterns; some species face extinction risk.

**4. Recent Developments & Key Reports (November 2023)**

* **IPCC Sixth Assessment Report (AR6):** Published in stages throughout 2021-2023, this report provides the most comprehensive assessment of climate change to date. It concludes with extremely high confidence that human influence is the dominant cause of observed warming. Key findings include:
    *  "It is unequivocal that human influence has warmed the atmosphere, ocean, and land."
    *  The worst impacts of climate change are now unavoidable.
