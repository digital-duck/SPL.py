## Step 4 — LLM intro (at 2026-06-13T10:11:42)
Eigenvalues and eigenvectors are special scalar-vector pairs associated with a square matrix that reveal its most fundamental geometric behavior: when a matrix acts on certain vectors, the direction of those vectors is preserved and only their magnitude is scaled. Formally, for a square matrix **A**, an eigenvector **v** is a nonzero vector satisfying **Av** = λ**v**, where the scalar λ is the corresponding eigenvalue — intuitively, the matrix simply "stretches" or "flips" that vector by a factor of λ. Finding these pairs is central to applications ranging from principal component analysis to solving systems of differential equations, and the process begins by solving the characteristic equation det(**A** − λ**I**) = 0. The worked example below uses the matrix A = [[4, 1], [2, 3]].
