Answer:
Alexander Graham Bell is widely credited with inventing the telephone. He was awarded the first patent for the telephone on March 7, 1876 (U.S. Patent 174,465), and made the first successful telephone call on March 10, 1876, when he famously said to his assistant Thomas Watson, "Mr. Watson, come here, I want to see you."

However, the invention of the telephone involved several inventors working on similar technologies around the same time:

- **Elisha Gray** filed a patent caveat for a telephone design on the same day as Bell's patent application, leading to a famous patent controversy
- **Antonio Meucci** had demonstrated voice transmission devices earlier and is sometimes credited as an earlier inventor, though he didn't secure a patent
- **Johann Philipp Reis** created an early sound transmission device called the "Reis telephone" in the 1860s

While Bell received the patent and commercial success, the development of telephone technology was the result of multiple inventors' contributions during the 1870s. Bell's design was the first to be successfully patented and commercialized, making him the person most commonly associated with the invention of the telephone.
