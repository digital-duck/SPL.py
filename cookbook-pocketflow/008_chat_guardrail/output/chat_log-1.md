## When to Go
- **Best:** April–June (mild, duck boats, cherry blossoms) or Sept–Oct (fall foliage, less crowded than summer)
- **Avoid:** January–February unless you enjoy brutal cold and ice

## Getting There & Around
- **Fly into:** Logan International (BOS) — 15 min by Silver Line bus to downtown (free from terminal)
- **Within the city:** Walk or take the MBTA "T" (oldest subway in North America). Get a CharlieCard for easy tap-on.
- **Skip renting a car** — parking is expensive and the street layout is infamously confusing

## Top Things to Do
- **Freedom Trail** — 2.5-mile red-brick walking path through 16 historic sites (free, self-guided)
- **Fenway Park** — Even if not a game day, tours are available; a classic American ballpark
- **Harvard & MIT** — Free to walk through; MIT museum is worth an hour
- **Isabella Stewart Gardner Museum** — Stunning Venetian-style palazzo with an unsolved $500M art heist backstory
- **Boston Public Garden** — Swan boats in spring/summer, gorgeous in fall
- **Quincy Market / Faneuil Hall** — Touristy but worth a stop for lobster rolls

## Food & Drink
- **Must-eat:** Clam chowder (Legal Sea Foods or Barking Crab), lobster roll, cannoli from Mike's Pastry (North End)
- **Neighborhoods to eat in:** North End (Italian), South End (farm-to-table), Chinatown (late night)

## Practical Tips
- Boston is very walkable — comfortable shoes are essential
- Many museums are free on select days (check ahead)
- The North End is the Italian neighborhood — worth the short walk from downtown
- Locals are called "Bostonians" and they pronounce it "Bah-ston" — don't say "Bawston"

## Sample 3-Day Itinerary
**Day 1:** Freedom Trail → North End lunch → Beacon Hill stroll → Fenway area dinner  
**Day 2:** Harvard/MIT in Cambridge → Kendall Square → Back Bay (Newbury St + Copley Square)  
**Day 3:** Isabella Gardner Museum → South End brunch → Boston Public Garden → Quincy Market
